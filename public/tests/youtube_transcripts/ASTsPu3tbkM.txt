HTS codes or harmonized tariff schedule codes are a standardized system of numbers used to classify goods for import into the United States. HTS codes are 10 digits long. The first six digits match the globally recognized harmonized system codes. The last four digits provide additional details specific to US imports. US Customs and Border Protection officials use HTS codes to determine the correct tariff rates for imported goods. Decide which regulatory requirements apply. It's easy to confuse HTS codes with other classification systems like HS codes and schedule B codes. So, let's clarify the differences. HS codes are sixdigit international standards used by over 200 countries to classify products. The first six digits of an HTS code are based on the HS code. HS codes are used globally for imports and exports. HTS codes are 10digit codes used only for imports into the US. The additional fourdigits provide more specific product information for US imports. Schedule B codes are 10digit codes used only for US exports. They are maintained by the US Census Bureau for statistical purposes. The first six digits match HS codes, but the final digits may differ from HTS codes. If you import and export the same product, you can use the HTS code for both unless the Census Bureau specifies otherwise.
