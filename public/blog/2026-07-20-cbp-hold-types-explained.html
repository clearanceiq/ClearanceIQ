CBP Hold Types Explained: EXAM, MAP, FDA, USDA, PGA

July 20, 2026 · Estimated reading: 6–8 minutes

The hold code tells you exactly what CBP wants. Match the code to the fix and your shipment moves. Here is the decoder.

EXAM — Intensive Exam

CBP wants to physically inspect the cargo. Your broker books the exam at the container station. Fix: nothing to file — just wait for the slot and make sure commercial documents are consistent so the exam doesn't expand. Most EXAMs clear in a few days once scanned.

MAP — Marking / Appraisement

CBP questions the value or country of origin. Fix: send proof of value (payment records, bank wires) and a correct origin declaration. Misvalued or mis-marked goods draw penalties, so respond with documentation, not assertions.

FDA — Food, Drugs, Devices, Cosmetics

Food, supplements, cosmetics, and devices need FDA before release. Fix: facility registration, Prior Notice, and (for food) an FSVP plan. Missing prior notice = automatic hold. File through your broker's FDA channel same day.

USDA / APHIS — Plant & Animal Products

Wood, seeds, leather, and food of animal origin may need a phytosanitary certificate or APHIS permit. Fix: supply the certificate from origin and the APHIS permit number. As of Jan 2026, Lacey Act PPQ 505 is electronic-only.

PGA — Partner Government Agencies

Covers everything from CPSC (children's products) to EPA (TSCA chemicals) to NHTSA (vehicles). Fix: identify the specific PGA and file its form (e.g., TSCA certification on the invoice, CPSC certificate). The broker must report the correct PGA data on the entry.

Missing bond

Not a "hold type" but the most common freeze. Fix: file a continuous or single-entry bond. Nothing releases until the bond is active.
